# Product

## Register

product

## Users

ABA (Applied Behavior Analysis) clinic staff in three roles:
- **Admins** — oversee the full client pipeline, manage staff, track authorizations
- **BCBAs** (Board Certified Behavior Analysts) — own clinical decisions, assessments, treatment plans
- **RBTs** (Registered Behavior Technicians) — deliver direct therapy, execute session work

Context: desktop-first, used during active caseload management — checking pipeline status, advancing clients through stages, assigning staff, uploading documents. Not a heads-down data-entry tool; it's the control panel they glance at between clinical sessions.

## Product Purpose

ABA Shield is a clinical CRM for ABA therapy centers. It manages the full client lifecycle from referral intake through insurance authorization, staffing, and active services. Core workflows: pipeline Kanban across 8 stages, checklist-gated stage advancement, staff cert tracking, document management, case notes, and reauthorization alerts.

Success looks like: zero clients falling through the cracks, admins knowing at a glance what needs attention, and BCBAs spending less time on administrative friction.

## Brand Personality

Calm, clinical, trustworthy.

The interface should feel like a well-organized binder in a confident clinician's hands — everything in its place, nothing surprising, information delivered without drama. Authority without rigidity.

## Anti-references

- **Generic EHR / Epic**: dense, gray-heavy, typographically flat, form-first. ABA Shield should feel modern without abandoning clinical seriousness.
- Bubbly consumer health apps: pastel gradients, rounded blobs, "friendly" at the cost of precision.
- Startup-dark dashboards: neon accents, metric-card grids, dark mode for aesthetic reasons.
- Corporate HR software: navy + gray compliance-tool aesthetic.

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity over cleverness** — every element earns its place by reducing cognitive load, not by demonstrating design skill.
2. **Status at a glance** — the most important question at any moment is "what needs attention?" Surface that answer before anything else.
3. **Clinical confidence** — the visual language should instill trust in a regulated environment. Measured, precise, never playful.
4. **Progressive disclosure** — show what matters now; reveal detail on demand. Checklists expand, notes thread, stages open.
5. **Respect the workflow** — users are between clinical sessions. The tool gets out of the way fast.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Keyboard navigation throughout. High-contrast focus states. No color-only status signals (pair with icons or labels). Reduced-motion support for animations.
